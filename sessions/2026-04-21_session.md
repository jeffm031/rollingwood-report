## Bug discovered at the real-send boundary

The preview email surfaced a citation-misalignment bug: YouTube
timestamp citations in the summary don't consistently point at the
moment each cited claim was made. Jeff cross-checked against the raw
transcript and verified the pattern. The LLM applies
section-anchor citations rather than claim-anchor citations —
one real timestamp gets reused across multiple distinct claims,
sometimes by different speakers at different moments. The outputs
are real timestamps (not hallucinated) but misaligned.

Full evidence in `sessions/2026-04-22_timestamp_audit.md`. The
accountability model depends on citations being verifiable, so
timestamp accuracy is non-negotiable for beta. The NOTES.md
prompt-tuning entry is now HIGH / beta-launch blocker.

## Meta observation

**Consumer-boundary testing catches failure modes that producer-side
testing can't simulate.** The dry-run rendered cleanly on paper —
HTML well-formed, links intact, no exceptions. The real-send
test landed the rendered email in front of a human reader (Jeff)
for the first time, and the human immediately noticed that the
citation links, while technically valid, pointed at the wrong
moments. That failure mode was invisible to every producer-side
check: dry-run stdout, HTML rendering in isolation, markdown-to-HTML
library behavior. It only surfaced at the first moment output
reached a reader.

Worth generalizing: **consumer-boundary tests — the first time
output lands in front of a real reader, even if that reader is
yourself — catch failure modes that producer-side tests can't
simulate.** The pattern applies to the first beta reader receiving
the first beta issue, and to whoever audits the first prompt-tuned
summary after the fix lands. Worth naming explicitly so the lesson
transfers forward, and worth structuring future chunks so that a
consumer-boundary check is part of the definition of "done" for
any output-generating change.
